Auction Market Theory — the foundation
The WHY behind everything. Understand this before any model.
Balance — do not trade breakouts here
70% fail

The market spends 70% of its time in balance. Price bounces between VAH and VAL with POC as the centre magnet. 70% of all breakout attempts from inside balance fail and snap back. Trading those breakouts = fighting the odds every time.

Imbalance — trade here only
LVN entry

When one side becomes dominant and price moves away from value with clear momentum, the market is seeking new fair value. This is the only condition you should enter trades. Find the LVN inside the impulse and enter on the pullback with footprint confirmation.

3-step AMT check — run this before any model
A · Market State
Is price in balance or out? In balance = do not trade. Only proceed when price clearly moves away from value with momentum.
B · Location
Draw Fixed Range VP over the impulse leg. Find the LVN inside it. Set an alert just BEFORE the LVN — never a blind limit order at it.
C · Aggression
Price at LVN: wait for big orders on footprint. NY: 30–40+ contracts. London: 20–30. No big print = no trade. Always.
Step 1 — Bias
1
Determine Bias — before looking at anything
VWAP and PSVP must both agree. One says long, other says short = wait.
VWAP — Volume Weighted Average Price
What is VWAP?
Average price of every trade today weighted by volume. Institutions benchmark against it — buying below, selling above. The session's gravity line. Resets daily and updates in real time.
LONG — price clearly above VWAP
SHORT — price clearly below VWAP
Choppy at VWAP = no clear bias = no trade. Wait for price to commit to one side.
PSVP — Previous Session Volume Profile
What is PSVP?
Yesterday's complete volume profile. VAH, POC and VAL carry into today because institutions still hold positions from those prices. Set to NOT extend right. Mark levels as manual horizontal lines on today's chart.
LONG — above PSVP VAH or POC
SHORT — below PSVP VAL or POC
Long Bias
VWAP above ✓
PSVP above VAH/POC ✓
Longs only.
Short Bias
VWAP below ✓
PSVP below VAL/POC ✓
Shorts only.
Conflict
Disagree or choppy at VWAP.
Wait. Do nothing.

LVN — Low Volume Node AMT KEY

A gap in the volume profile where almost no one traded. Price moves very fast through LVNs because there's nothing to slow it down. When price pulls back into an LVN during a trend — that's your precision entry zone.

Model 1 — Absorption
One side attacks aggressively. The other side absorbs silently without letting price move. The aggressive side is trapped — their forced exits become your trade fuel. Key signal: large delta one direction, candle closes AGAINST that delta.
Short setup — Δ+ red close
Price taps sell zone (VAH, PSVP resistance, weekly level)
Footprint shows massive buying — but candle closes red or flat
Positive delta + red close = sellers won, buyers TRAPPED
Entry: reaction candle close short or limit at zone top
Stop: 15 ticks above trapped candle wick high
Target: balance POC or next LVN below
Long setup — Δ− green close
Price taps buy zone (VAL, PSVP support, shelf/ledge)
Footprint shows massive selling — but candle closes green
Negative delta + green close = buyers won, sellers TRAPPED
Entry: reaction candle close long or limit at zone
Stop: 15 ticks below trapped candle wick low
Target: balance POC or next LVN above
Model 2 — Breakout Continuation
Price breaks THROUGH a zone — not just tapping. The opposing side who faded the break gets trapped. Price retraces to the broken level (an LVN on Fixed Range VP). That retest is your entry. Never chase the initial break — wait for the retest.
Short setup
AMT: market breaking out of balance with displacement
Price breaks decisively THROUGH sell zone — not a tap
Sellers who faded the break are trapped above the zone
Price retraces to broken zone — this is the LVN on Fixed Range VP
Sell aggression returns at retest, trapped bulls must exit
Entry: reaction candle close at LVN/zone or limit
Stop: 15 ticks above breakout spike high
Target: next balance POC below
Long setup
Price breaks DOWN through buy zone, absorbs buyers below
Retraces back UP to broken level — LVN retest from below
Buy aggression returns, trapped sellers must exit
Entry: reaction candle close or limit at LVN
Stop: 15 ticks below breakdown spike low
Target: next balance POC above
Model 3 — Rejection
One side tries to break a level and gets completely denied. Opposite side steps in the instant price touches the zone. Signal is sharp and visible on the candle alone: long wick into zone, immediate reversal. AMT: happens at balance edges — market tested outside value and snapped back.
Short setup
AMT: testing VAH edge from inside balance
Buyers push into sell zone — sellers immediately deny, zero follow-through
Long upper wick into zone, closes far below
Entry: rejection candle close short or limit
Stop: 15 ticks above rejection wick high
Target: balance POC — return to fair value
Long setup
AMT: testing VAL edge from inside balance
Sellers push into buy zone — buyers immediately deny
Long lower wick into zone, closes far above
Entry: rejection candle close long or limit
Stop: 15 ticks below rejection wick low
Target: balance POC
Rejection is FAST — visible on candle alone. Absorption is SLOW — price barely moves but delta tells the story on footprint. Very different signals.
Model 4 — Acceptance
Price taps a zone and instead of rejecting — it STAYS. Multiple small candles consolidate at the level. The market is agreeing this is fair value. Sellers at resistance (or buyers at support) are building positions. The consolidation IS the signal. Enter on the break from the consolidation range.
Short setup
AMT: market accepting new lower value
Price taps sell zone — multiple small candles AT the level, no bounce
Delta stays negative — sellers building
Entry: reaction candle close on break DOWN from consolidation
Stop: 15 ticks above consolidation high
Target: next LVN below or next balance POC
Long setup
AMT: market accepting new higher value
Price taps buy zone — small tight candles, no selloff
Delta positive or flipping positive — buyers building
Entry: reaction candle close on break UP from consolidation
Stop: 15 ticks below consolidation low
Target: next LVN above or balance POC
Longer price consolidates without rejecting = stronger acceptance = more powerful continuation. Time at zone = conviction.
Model 5 — AMT Trend Model FROM EPISODES
Market is out of balance, pushing directionally. Draw Fixed Range VP over the impulse to find the LVN. Enter on pullback to LVN with big order confirmation. Best in NY session. Target = next balance POC.
Full sequence
Step 1 — Confirm OUT OF BALANCE. Clear displacement from prior value with momentum. If rotating, skip.
Step 2 — Draw Fixed Range VP from start to end of impulse leg.
Step 3 — Find LVN inside that leg. Set alert BEFORE it — never a blind limit at it.
Step 4 — Price pulls back to LVN: check footprint for big prints in trend direction.
Step 5 — NY: 30–40+ contracts. London: 20–30. No big print = stay flat.
Stop — Just beyond aggressive print + 1–2 tick buffer. Not at obvious swing high.
Target — Previous balance POC. Exit full position. 70% reverse at balance.
Breakeven — Strong CVD after entry → move stop to BE early.
Runners — Strong trend: trail to next HTF level (prior day H/L, overnight extreme).
NY session only. Avoid London open — too many fake breakouts.
Model 6 — AMT Mean Reversion FROM EPISODES
Market in balance tries to break out but FAILS. No follow-through. Market wants to return to balance POC. Wait for: failed break → clear reclaim inside balance → pullback into reclaim LVN → footprint aggression. Best in London or compressed conditions.
Full sequence
Step 1 — Market IN BALANCE. Use prior day's profile as reference.
Step 2 — Price pushes out of balance and FAILS. First failure — do NOT enter.
Step 3 — Wait for CLEAR RECLAIM back inside balance + pullback into the reclaim leg.
Step 4 — Draw VP on reclaim leg. Mark the LVNs inside it.
Step 5 — At LVN: big buy prints for longs, big sell prints for shorts. Aggression = enter.
Stop — Just beyond the aggressive print. Wrong = wrong immediately.
Target — Balance POC. Exit full position. No stretching for other side.
Never take first move back inside balance. Always wait for the full sequence: failed break → clear reclaim → pullback → LVN → footprint aggression.

What is GEX?
GEX stands for Gamma Exposure. Think of it as a map of where big options dealers are forced to buy or sell to stay hedged. When price moves toward a high-GEX level, dealers push back — creating magnets, walls, and flip points. You don't need to understand options to use this. Just read the plain-English summary below.

GEX Concepts — plain definitions
Gamma Flip
The price level where dealers switch from being buyers of dips (supportive) to sellers of rallies (suppressive). Above the flip = market is cushioned. Below the flip = moves accelerate.
Call Wall
The strike with the highest concentration of call options. Dealers are short calls here, so they sell the underlying as price approaches — creating a ceiling effect.
Put Wall
The strike with the highest put concentration. Dealers are long puts, so they buy the underlying as price falls toward it — creating a floor effect.
Positive vs Negative GEX Regime
Positive GEX: Dealers act as shock absorbers — they buy dips and sell rips automatically, dampening volatility and keeping price range-bound.
Negative GEX: Dealers are forced to follow price — they buy when it goes up and sell when it goes down, amplifying moves and increasing volatility.
